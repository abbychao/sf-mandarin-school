The Demand Is Real

Why This School Matters

The Legacy

San Francisco Led the Way

Our city has a proud legacy of language immersion education. Alice Fong Yu was the first public Chinese immersion school in the country and Chinese American International School was the first Mandarin immersion school of any kind in the nation.

The Crisis

Tremendous Demand, Too Few Seats

Every year, roughly 400 families apply for just 66 seats for Mandarin Immersion Kindergarten at Starr King and Jose Ortega elementary schools. The demand far outpaces supply, and families are turned away every year.

Now → September 2026

Making It Real

SFUSD has committed to opening a dedicated Mandarin Immersion TK–8 school in fall 2027. 700+ families have already expressed interest. Liana Szeto, founding principal of Alice Fong Yu, is leading the charge. The board must formally approve the school by September 2026. This is our window.

Our Work

Building With SFUSD

We hold monthly meetings with district leadership. Together, we’ve identified the areas where our group can have the most impact:

Parent Engagement

Keeping families connected and informed about progress, timelines, and ways to get involved as the school takes shape.

Teacher Recruiting

Finding exceptional Mandarin immersion educators who share our vision for world-class bilingual education. View the job description →

Fundraising

Rallying community resources to ensure the school opens with the programs, materials, and support our children deserve.

Driving Urgency

Pushing SFUSD to meet its September 2026 Board deadline — the vote that determines whether the school opens in 2027–2028.
