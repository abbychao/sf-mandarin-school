    Why This School Matters

    The Legacy

    San Francisco Led the Way

    Our city has a proud legacy of language immersion education. Alice Fong Yu was the first public Chinese immersion school in the country and Chinese American International School was the first Mandarin immersion school of any kind in the nation.

    The Crisis

    Tremendous Demand, Too Few Seats

    Every year, roughly 400 families apply for just 66 seats for Mandarin Immersion Kindergarten at Starr King and Jose Ortega elementary schools. The demand far outpaces supply, and families are turned away every year.

    Now → September 2026

    Making It Real

    SFUSD has committed to opening a dedicated Mandarin Immersion K–8 school in fall 2027. 400+ families have already expressed interest. Liana Szeto, founding principal of Alice Fong Yu, is leading the charge. The board must formally approve the school by September 2026. This is our window.

    Our Work

    Parents and community members working across four areas to bring SF's Mandarin immersion school to life.

    Keeping families connected and informed about progress, timelines, and ways to get involved as the school takes shape.

    Teacher Recruiting

    Finding exceptional Mandarin immersion educators who share our vision for a world-class bilingual education.

    Fundraising

    Rallying community resources to ensure the school opens with the programs, materials, and support our children deserve.

    Driving Urgency

    Holding SFUSD accountable to its commitment and making sure the 2027–2028 opening stays on track.
